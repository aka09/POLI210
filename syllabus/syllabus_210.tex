\hypertarget{what-is-this-course-for}{%
\section{What is this course for?}\label{what-is-this-course-for}}

This course is an introduction to research methods in political science.
It will cover a broad range of topics, including questions of philosophy
of science; causality and the difficult enterprise of causal inference;
how to ask empirical questions about the political world (and which ones
to ask!); formulating empirically testable hypotheses; measurement and
data collection using surveys and interviews; and qualitative and
quantitative data analysis techniques designed to test your hypotheses.

\hypertarget{pre-requisites-formal-and-informal}{%
\subsection{Pre-requisites -- formal and
informal}\label{pre-requisites-formal-and-informal}}

Formal: There are no pre-requisites for enrolling in this course.
However, if you have already taken POLI311, you are not eligible to take
this course. For those who matriculated in the fall of 2017 or later,
POLI210 serves as a prerequisite for POLI311.

Informal: You do \emph{not} need to have a math/stats background to
succeed in this course. We will provide you with the tools to succeed
regardless of your background.

\hypertarget{learning-outcomes}{%
\subsection{Learning outcomes}\label{learning-outcomes}}

\begin{itemize}
\tightlist
\item
  Become critical consumers of political science research and empirical
  claims in popular media.
\item
  Acquire knowledge of important quantitative and qualitative empirical
  methods in the study of politics.
\item
  Become acquainted with \emph{R} -- a free, open-source software that
  is highly marketable (this very syllabus and all course slides were
  created using \emph{R}!)
\end{itemize}

\hypertarget{textbook}{%
\subsection{Textbook}\label{textbook}}

The textbook for this course will be:

Berdahl, Loleen and Jason Roy. \emph{Explorations: Conducting Empirical
Research in Canadian Political Science} (4th edition). Oxford University
Press.

You can purchase the print version at the McGill bookstore or rent a
copy at \href{https://www.redshelf.com}{redshelf}. Note that we will be
using the newer 4th edition. If you use an older edition of the
textbook, it is incumbent upon you to make sure that you are not missing
important material.

\hypertarget{other-resources}{%
\subsection{Other resources}\label{other-resources}}

People learn in different ways and this is \emph{especially true} when
it comes to research methods. What works for me may not work for you!
Fortunately, there are a lot of resources you can use:

\begin{itemize}
\tightlist
\item
  StackOverflow is a Q\&A forum for programmers. If you type a question
  related to \emph{R} in Google, chances are that a StackOverflow thread
  will show up!
\item
  Thomas J. Leeper taught a similar course at LSE (the content of this
  very course is inspired by his). You can view the syllabus and slides
  on \href{https://thomasleeper.com/designcourse/}{his personal
  website}.
\item
  \emph{Quantitative Social Science: An Introduction} by Kosuke Imai is
  an excellent textbook that can help you learn \emph{R} and principles
  of statistics.
\item
  \href{https://r4ds.had.co.nz/index.html}{\emph{R for Data Science}} is
  a free online book that introduces you to the basics of
  \emph{R}/\texttt{tidyverse}.
\end{itemize}

\hypertarget{labs-r-and-rstudio}{%
\section{Labs: R and RStudio}\label{labs-r-and-rstudio}}

In labs, which are expected to take place in person, you will be
introduced to quantitative data analysis using the statistical software
\emph{R}, a free and open-source program. We will also be using RStudio,
which is a graphical user interface that makes learning R easier. You
can download \emph{R} \href{http://www.cran.r-project.org}{here}; note
that you need to choose the version that matches your operating system.
You can download RStudio
\href{https://www.rstudio.com/products/rstudio/download/}{here}. Try to
do this as soon as possible! You will need \emph{R} to complete most of
the assignments. Your presence in labs is essential to your success in
this course -- this is a skill that can only be learned by doing!

\hypertarget{optional-video-clips}{%
\section{Optional video clips}\label{optional-video-clips}}

In an effort to make the course materials more tangible, I will
occasionally produce short (and hopefully fun) videos that approach
things from a more informal angle. These are completely optional! This
course -- and other methods courses -- is an excellent gateway into
deeper, philosophical questions about the nature of knowledge. The aim
is to ease your entry into the world of empirical political science by
covering fun, real-world topics that would usually not be covered in a
course like this. Some of the topics we may cover include: recent
controversies in political science, the history of philosophy of
science, how norms vary across disciplines in the social sciences, how
the publication process works, what an efficient data analysis workflow
looks like\ldots Please feel free to suggest a different topic you would
like to hear about!

\hypertarget{evaluation-policy}{%
\section{Evaluation policy}\label{evaluation-policy}}

\begin{itemize}
\tightlist
\item
  Assignments: 45\%. There will be 5 assignments; assignment 1 is worth
  5 points; assignments 2 through 5 are worth 10 points each. You are
  allowed to talk generally about the problem sets with your classmates,
  but you are expected to complete them individually. \emph{You cannot
  share code with your classmates}.

  \begin{itemize}
  \tightlist
  \item
    Assignment 1, due on September 20th at 11:59pm, will simply ask to
    fill in a class survey on the platform \emph{Qualtrics}. The data
    you provide here will be of use to create some interactive examples
    in class!
  \item
    Assignment 2, due on October 7th at 11:59pm, will ask you to perform
    basic operations in \emph{R} using the class survey: compute
    measures of central tendency, recoding variables, describing
    distributions.
  \item
    Assignment 3, due on October 25th at 11:59pm, will ask you to work
    with experimental data from a famous political science article. It
    will address issues of causality.
  \item
    Assignment 4, due on November 1st at 11:59pm, will focus on the
    case-study and comparative approaches.
  \item
    Assignment 5, due on November 26th at 11:59pm, will ask you to work
    with data from the 2019 Canadian Election Study and will focus on
    comparisons between means, p-values, multivariate regression.
  \end{itemize}
\item
  Quizzes: 15\%. The first quiz will be posted to the ``Quiz'' section
  on MyCourses on Monday October 25th and you will need to complete it
  by Wednesday October 27th, 11:59pm. The second quiz will be posted on
  Tuesday November 30th and you will need to complete it by Thursday
  December 2nd, 11:59pm. Each is worth 7.5\%. Anything covered in
  lectures or in the readings is fair game; the format is multiple
  choice questions. Once you have opened the quiz, you will have 1.5
  hour to complete it.
\item
  Midterm: 20\%. The midterm will be posted on MyCourses on Tuesday
  November 9th and you will need to submit your answers by Friday
  November 12th, 11:59pm. Everything up to the material from week 10
  (inclusively) is fair game. You are allowed to use any course
  materials, but you are expected to complete the midterm on an
  individual basis. Once you have opened the midterm, you will have 3
  hours to complete it.
\item
  Team project: 20\%. Due on December 6th at 11:59pm. In teams of four,
  you will choose a political science article from a pre-approved list
  that will span the subfields of the discipline as well as the
  methodological approaches we will have covered in this course. You
  will first have to answer basic questions about the article: what is
  the research question? What is the hypothesis? Then, you will be asked
  to take a more critical approach and assess the methodological merits
  of the article. What are the main threats to inference and how well do
  the authors defend their research design? More details will be given
  in class in November.
\end{itemize}
